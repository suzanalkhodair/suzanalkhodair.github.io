The Editing Process

What exactly does an editor do?

Let's begin by clarifying the difference between editing and proofreading. Editing involves thoroughly reading a piece and improving its content for clarity, comprehension, conciseness, and other elements. Proofreading, on the other hand, is a reading of the manuscript to correct spelling and punctuation mistakes as well as errors missed during the editing step.

As your editor, I do both while also checking the format. I will read your manuscript from start to finish—along with all elements like tables, figures, and references—to check that it has all the elements recommended for the type of paper.
