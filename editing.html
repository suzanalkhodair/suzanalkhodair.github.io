The Editing Process

What exactly does an editor do?

Let's begin by clarifying the difference between editing and proofreading. Editing involves thoroughly reading a piece and improving its content for clarity, comprehension, conciseness, and other elements. Proofreading, on the other hand, is a reading of the manuscript to correct spelling and punctuation mistakes as well as errors missed during the editing step.

As your editor, I do both while also checking the format. I will read your manuscript from start to finish—along with all elements like tables, figures, and references—to check that it has all the elements recommended for the type of paper. In most cases I would review a manuscript multiple time, each time focusing on a different element. Things I look for include the following:

  • Is the paper easy to read?
  • Are there difficult-to-read sentences that can be simplified?
  • Do the ideas flow logically?
  • Do there seem to be missing parts?
  • Are there obvious errors in the numbers?
  • Are referencing and citing done correctly?
  • Are quotations used correctly?
  • Language and style
  • Grammar and punctuation
  • Correct use of terms and phrases
  • Is the language used appropriate for the target reader?
  • Are there inappropriate terms?
  • homonyms
  • Abbreviations and acronyms

Steps

Editing usually goes through two steps at least (more in certain cases)

After the first edit, I add comments and questions (when needed) and send the file back so you can review them and reply to questions. The file is then returned to me for the final proofread, which targets the following:

  • Spelling errors; American or British
  • Capitalization
  • Use of hyphens and dashes
  • Punctuation
  • Numbers
  • Abbreviations
  • Extra or missing spaces

Format

Format is one of the important elements of any manuscript or thesis. I can format your manuscript following the instructions of any journal you choose. Theses and dissertations can be styled as per APA or university guidelines. If no specific format is chosen, it is important to at least follow a consistent style throughout the paper. Among the elements for format are:

  • Font type, size, and color
  • Headings and their levels
  • Tables and figures
  • Table of contents
  • References/citations
